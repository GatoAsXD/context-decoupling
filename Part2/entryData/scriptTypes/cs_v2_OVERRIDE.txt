OBJECTIVE
Evaluate late-stage constraint handling and conflict resolution. The user establishes a stable context initially but introduces a critical constraint late in the conversation that alters or invalidates prior assumptions.

STRUCTURE
Phase 1 (≈60%): Stable development under implicit assumptions.
- The user and assistant develop the task or topic normally.
- There are no hidden constraints; the progression is consistent and logical.
- The conversation suggests a clear direction or feasibility based on initial information.

Phase 2 (≈25%): Introduce a new major constraint.
- The user introduces a new, explicit constraint that changes the feasibility, direction, or requirements of the task.
- This constraint acts as an "override" to the assumptions made in Phase 1.
- It must be clearly stated but not accompanied by meta-commentary (e.g., do not say "ignore what I said earlier").

Phase 3 (≈15%): Final request that must respect the new constraint.
- The final question depends primarily on the late constraint.
- The assistant must reconcile prior reasoning and adapt the output without ignoring the new requirement or creating internal contradictions.

CONTENT & LINGUISTIC CONSTRAINTS

The Constraint: Should feel realistic (e.g., a sudden budget cut, a new dietary restriction, a change in timeline).
No Meta-commentary: Do not explicitly instruct the model to delete memory; the constraint itself should force the adaptation.
Resolution: The final output must demonstrate that the late constraint takes precedence over early assumptions.
