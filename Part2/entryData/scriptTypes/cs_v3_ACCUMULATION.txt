OBJECTIVE
Evaluate incremental memory construction and synthesis. The user does not dump information at once but distributes critical facts gradually across the entire conversation, requiring the assistant to aggregate them.

STRUCTURE
Phase 1 (≈30%): General framing and exploration.
- The user introduces the general objective or topic.
- No critical constraints are established yet; the assistant responds to general inquiries.

Phase 2 (≈40%): Gradual injection of important new facts.
- The user adds important facts or constraints one by one across multiple turns.
- At least 3 important constraints must appear in different parts of this phase.
- Crucial: No single message contains all key facts, and facts are not repeated once stated.

Phase 3 (≈30%): Final request requiring synthesis of all prior facts.
- The user requests a final output that requires the integration of all dispersed information.
- There should be no explicit summary provided by the user.
- The assistant must combine the scattered constraints to generate the correct response.

CONTENT & LINGUISTIC CONSTRAINTS
- Motivation: Each constraint injection should feel contextually motivated, avoiding a "list-like" exposition.
- Completeness: The final evaluation focuses on the absence of partial omissions; the model must recall all dispersed pieces.
- Tone: Natural progression where details emerge as the conversation evolves.
