OBJECTIVE
Evaluate early-context dependency. The user introduces critical constraints at the very beginning of the conversation (precise numbers, dates, or preferences) and expects consistent adherence throughout, despite a long gap of unrelated conversation.

STRUCTURE
Phase 1 (≈20%): Introduce key personal constraints or situational conditions.
- The user introduces concrete, specific facts or formatting requirements that will be relevant later.
- These facts must clearly establish 2–4 constraints and appear only in the first messages.
- Crucial: No repetition, rephrasing, or reinforcement of these facts after this phase.

Phase 2 (≈50%): Develop topic naturally with sub-questions and mild evolution.
- Introduce multiple unrelated topics such as facts, opinions, advice requests, or trivia.
- These topics must be semantically independent from the early facts (distractors).
- The majority of the script belongs to this phase to create "distance" in token space.
- Avoid any accidental reference to early facts.

Phase 3 (≈30%): Ask final questions that implicitly depend on early constraints.
- The user asks questions that require the assistant to recall the early constraints to answer correctly.
- The request may introduce a subtle conflicting suggestion to test adherence.
- Do not explicitly mention memory, recall, or use phrases like "as said before".

CONTENT & LINGUISTIC CONSTRAINTS

Early facts: Must be precise (numbers, names, specific constraints) and non-obvious (not inferable from general knowledge).
Tone: Natural conversational tone throughout.
References: Indirect references only; never restate constraints verbatim in later turns.
Distractors: Must not semantically overlap with early facts or feel like variations of the same topic.
